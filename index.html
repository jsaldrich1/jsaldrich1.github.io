Playing Cards

I'm currently designing a deck of standard playing cards with a few additions. Inspired by a friend of mine who introduced me to a game he called Wizards. The game plays similar to Hearts, by betting how many tricks you can take in a given hand, however there's a twist. There are four extra cards - the Wizards - which of course, no standard deck of 52 come with. My friend had made his own deck by buying two identical decks and adding the four aces, with some Sharpie based alterations, to the first deck. This seemed to work, but it got me thinking about designing up a deck of cards complete with the Wizards so we wouldn't have to deal with the scribbled cards. Updates to follow.

  • Client: Personal Friend
  • Date: September 2016
  • Service: Print Design

Patrick the Pine

A friend of mine came up with a story for a children's book a number of years ago, but never really did anything with it. Between school, work and planning his wedding he didn't exactly have the time. After he was married, they immediately had a set of twins - adorable I might add. It was their first birthday that reminded me to dig up the story he had written down and left me to illustrate. I teamed up with my equally ambitious artist girlfriend and we got to work tweaking the storyboard, illustrating and finalizing the book's design. We had a single copy printed for the family and set a standard for gift giving I'm sure will never come back to bite me.

  • Client: Personal Friend
  • Date: April 2016
  • Service: Print Design

High to Low

High to Low was a mobile game idea I came up with where the player would tap a number of related items in a certain order. The gimmick was that the order I had the user thinking about wasn't a very typical one. For instance, one particular game might display the numbers 1 through 9 and ask you to order the numbers alphabetically A to Z... think Eight, Five, Four...

I designed and developed the game entirely using Adobe Flash CS6 and Adobe AIR. I published High to Low to the Google Play Store in September of 2012, but it became apparent after a year that maintaining a Flash game on Android was just not the right way to go.

  • Client: Self
  • Date: Released September 2012
  • Service: Adobe Flash, AS3

Gym Bros

A board and/or card game idea in which players would each take control of a unique gym bro and become the biggest dude in the jungle. Players may draw and play funny, gym and bro related cards, roll dice and occupy different positions on the board (equipment in the gym) in order to get the most gains themselves, while also deflating their oponent's egos.

Jerbear Beer

THe brewing process was fine and all, but designing and printing the labels for my beers was the real fun part.
