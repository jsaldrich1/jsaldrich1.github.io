Playing Cards

I'm designing a deck of standard playing cards with a few additions. Inspired by a friend of mine who introduced me to a game he called Wizards. The game plays similar to Hearts but introduces an additional face card to each suite. Here you can see what I’ve imagined for the Wizards of Heart and Spade.

Patrick the Pine

Years ago, a friend of mine wrote a short story with his then fiancé for a children's book. They asked me to illustrate the story, but the project sat shelved for a couple years, during which my friends got married and had twins. The kid’s first birthday reinspired me to dust off the story and get to work. Had a single copy printed for the family, it’s never too early to learn to share.

Emails and Notifications

I created several visuals to better articulate the relationship that user actions had on generating notification emails for other users of the system.

This was crucial in early stages to allow the business and stakeholders to think about alternative notification mechanisims in the service, enabling the team to reprioratize work in this area of the product much sooner than previously allocated.

Bearmaggedon

OK. I’m going to be honest. This is a placeholder piece for a dream of mine. I’ve toyed with the idea of creating a 2D shoot-em-up platformer game for years. Without giving too much away, imagine a bear revolution and uprising. Kodiak and Grizzly. Brothers in arms. Taking back the forests from mankind. The project involves me doing a ludicrous amount of painting, animating and programing. As such, it has been a very, very slow process. Don’t worry about the details, just know it’s going to be awesome.

Jerbear Beer

As an avid beer lover, I decided to get into the hobby of craft brewing my own beer. I made two batches, a standard American Pilsner which I used honey as a sugar alternative and a Mexican style lagar that I followed by the books. The Lager turned out much, much better. THe brewing process was fine and all, but designing and printing the labels for my beers was the real fun part.
