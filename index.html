TechSmith Video Review

Emails and Notifications

To better communicate some user pain points in Video Review, I created some low fidelity diagrams which visualized the complexity of the system. It was much easier to have conversations with engineers and managers once everyone could see which actions in the system were generating email and just how often it was happening.

This was crucial in early stages to allow the business and stakeholders to think about alternative notification mechanisims in the service, enabling the team to pivot and begin work on condensing information into a daily summary, as well as giving the user the ability to configure what, and how many, emails they receive from Video Review.

These changes directly addressed concerns and requests from beta users and should set the product up for better notifications in the future.

TSC Account Proposal


TechSmith ran an internal Hackathon of sorts, where leadership encouraged employees to focus on solving some existing customer pain points. I joined up with five others from various departments to talk about known problems with TechSmith Accounts. We only had about two days from start to finish. Knowing that, we began brainstorming major complaints and nuances so we could collect like things and focus on solving as much as we could with limited time and resources.



We decided to double down on getting customers to the data they often need most. We had resources on our team with insight to customer calls and tech support issues, so we used that to determine where and how to present information to the user. I drew up some low fidelity mockups on the whiteboard after taking in a lot of info and direction from the team. These mockups would be the base for the demo we constructed.



The team and I created an interactive mockup with Marvel to present as our final proposal. In the span of two days, we had collected real data based on our customers most common questions and issues and catalogued it alongside a highly detailed mockup which provided a simple answer to all of those issues. We spoke with our internal customer support teams and figure the changes we proposed could prevent hundreds of customer calls each day. The results were well received and the team is doing additional research to outline a more detailed proposal so the actual work can be done.

Telephone Drawing Game

The Telephone Drawing game is a party game that my group of friends loves to play when we get together. It’s a lot like Telestrations if you’ve ever played that before, but with a few twists.

One of these friends and I had talked about making the game as a mobile app, or browser game. Getting some tangible artifacts is crucial in actually driving any project forward. Doesn’t have to be fancy, something low fidelity works just fine for vetting ideas. For me that means whiteboarding and doodling, which I really like to do in Sketchbook. Pair those doodles with some quick prototyping tools and you end up with something magic.

Using Sketchbook, I was able to hammer out some rough ideas and hook everything up in Adobe XD to make this prototype in no time at all. Talk is great, but showing a prototype like this really gets the gears turning.
